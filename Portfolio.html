About Me

Howdy! I am a recent first-generation graduate from Texas A&M University. In the past four years, I have been studying human-environment interactions and gained a specific interest in the applications of GIS technologies in disaster science and emergency management. My sophomore year of undergrad I took a natural hazards course with a professor who researched disaster science and resilient communities. It was her passion that helped me realize that was something I would love to one day do. To apply my background in the geosciences and GIS, and help communities efficiently plan, mitigate any adverse effects, respond, and recover from disasters.
